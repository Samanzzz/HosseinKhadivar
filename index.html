About Me

Learn more about my background and research interests

Innovative Environmental Engineering Researcher

I am a PhD student in Environmental Engineering with a focus on developing Engineered Living Materials (ELMs) that harness the power of microorganisms to address environmental challenges. My research bridges synthetic biology, materials science, and environmental engineering.

As an NSF NRT Fellow at the Center for Biofilm Engineering, I work on creating novel biomaterials with self-healing, self-adapting, and environmentally responsive properties. These materials have applications in water treatment, bioremediation, sustainable construction, and environmental sensing.

My interdisciplinary approach aims to develop sustainable solutions that minimize ecological footprints while addressing pressing environmental issues through innovative biotechnology.
